Son sued for parricide awaits mental health check

Published on: Jan 22, 2025, 10:11 pm

The Tagum City Police Station has yet to conduct a medical investigation on the suspect who allegedly killed his own father by ripping his head off and took all of his internal organs .. The hacking incident transpired on January 18, 2025 at Purok 1A-Gapas, Barangay Madaum, Tagum City.

THE son who murdered his 60-year-old father in Purok 1A-Gapas, Barangay Madaum, Tagum City, has not yet undergone a psychiatric evaluation, as the decision depends on the court's ruling.

Tagum Police chief, Police Colonel Wildemar Tiu, said in a phone interview on Wednesday, January 22, 2025, that they are waiting for the judge's order to determine if the suspect will be evaluated for any mental health conditions.

“Mao pa ni sya ang amoang ginahulat pero naga-depende man gud ang kana [psychological evaluation] sa korte, kung unsa pud ang desisyon sa judge ana (This is what we are still waiting for, but the psychological evaluation depends on the court, depending on the judge's decision),” he said.

The suspect has been charged with parricide, filed by his brother on January 20.

Tiu confirmed that one of the suspect’s siblings filed the case with the prosecutor's office after the incident.

Authorities also confirmed that the suspect tested negative for drugs, dispelling online rumors.

However, Tiu noted that the suspect was not in a stable mental state when apprehended.

“Kinsa man pud tarong nga tao ang mubuhat ani. Mura na to syag cannibalism maong importante gyud pud mahibal-an kung naa ba syay mental condition (Who in their right mind would commit such an act? It almost seems like cannibalism, so it's essential to assess his mental health),” Tiu added.

The suspect confessed to hearing voices when committing the crime, which could indicate "schizophrenia," according to psychologist and professor De Mar Bangeles, though this has not been confirmed due to a lack of evidence.

Tiu also said that the suspect is being provided with food and basic needs during the investigation to aid his recovery and assist in the ongoing inquiry.

“Sa pagkakaron, amo sya ginapakaon kay para maulian pud sya ug maka-tabang sya sa amoang imbestigasyon (We're taking care of him so he can recover and help with our investigation),” he said.

Tagum Dialysis Center 97% complete, to offer free services

Published on: Jan 20, 2025, 12:43 am

The City Government of Tagum reported that the Diagnostic and Dialysis Center at the City Hall Grounds is now 97 percent complete. City Government of Tagum

THE City Government of Tagum reported that the Diagnostic and Dialysis Center at the City Hall Grounds is now 97 percent complete.

According to the latest update from the Department of Public Works and Highways (DPWH), the center is 97.01 percent finished. It will feature 24 dialysis units, laboratory equipment, and x-ray machines.

“Ang unang bahin niini kay pagalangkuban og 24 ka unit sa dialysis devices, laboratory equipment, ug x-ray machine, mga serbisyo nga libreng yakni alang sa mga katawhan (The first phase will include 24 dialysis devices, laboratory equipment, and an x-ray machine—services that will be offered free of charge to the people),” the LGU shared on Facebook on Friday, January 17.

The center will also house offices from various agencies offering social services to Tagumenyos, including PhilHealth, the Philippine Charity Sweepstakes Office (PCSO), and the Department of Social Welfare and Development (DSWD).

Tagum Mayor Rey T. Uy confirmed that the building is expected to be completed this year, making it easier for the public to access essential services.

Mayor Uy led the groundbreaking ceremony for the center on August 4, 2023. Located on a newly acquired lot next to the City Hall Complex, the P150 million infrastructure project is funded by the national government.

The National Kidney and Transplant Institute recently reported a 42 percent increase in the number of dialysis patients in the Philippines, from 25,125 in 2022 to 35,714 in 2023, with diabetes and hypertension—common lifestyle diseases—being significant contributors to the rise in cases. RGP

Tagumenya minor dies in road tragedy in Kidapawan

Published on: Published on: Dec 14, 2024, 2:06 am

A 16-YEAR-OLD girl from Tagum City was declared dead on arrival at the hospital after the motorcycle she was driving collided with a truck in Barangay San Isidro, Kidapawan City, around 2 p.m. on December 12, 2024.

The victim, identified as alias Myla, a resident of Barangay Cuambugan, was visiting her grandmother in Barangay San Isidro when the accident occurred. She was riding with a seven-year-old passenger, who also sustained injuries.

Reports indicate that the truck, driven by a 52-year-old man named Roland from Sudapin, Kidapawan City, and the motorcycle were traveling in opposite directions before the collision. The teen reportedly lost control of her motorcycle and veered into the truck's lane. Despite the truck driver’s attempt to avoid the crash, he was unable to do so due to the sudden nature of the incident.

Police have taken the truck driver into custody while continuing their investigation. JRN
